About GECTO

GECTO (Glorious Eland City Tech One) is the technology and software development division of GEC Industries, launched in early 2026 to drive digital transformation across Africa. We are not just a software company; we are a movement to replace paper‑based record‑keeping with intelligent databases, e‑commerce platforms, and custom mobile applications. Our unique edge is gamification – every business client receives a BrandQuest promotional game, and we build SkillForge educational games that teach real‑life skills to children and youth.

Rooted in African strength and visionary leadership, GECTO exists to empower underserved communities with technology. We offer website development, Android/iOS apps, system maintenance, security patching, graphic design, and game development. Our team is committed to excellence, precision, and future‑ready solutions. We believe that digital tools should be accessible, affordable, and impactful.

Since our founding, we have completed projects for real estate (Mzinyathi Gardens), churches (Dunamis Bible Church International), models, artists, and small businesses. We have delivered over 20 graphic design projects and built several custom web and mobile platforms. Our approach is client‑centric: we listen, design, build, and then support with lifetime maintenance.

GECTO's headquarters is currently in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe, with a future innovation hub planned for Gwanda by 2029. Our motto, "The one digitalised world," drives everything we do. We aim to contribute to the world's technological growth by ensuring that every sector – from agriculture to education, from retail to governance – operates within a seamless, data‑driven, and efficient digital sphere. Join us as we build digital legacies across Africa and beyond.

Meet Our Founder

Stenford Mpofu (Mr Ceo)

Born in Gwanda and raised in Beitbridge, Stenford completed his studies in Bulawayo. In 2022, he founded GEC Industries, a legacy‑driven company focused on creating value for Zimbabwean communities. Seeing the gap in digital adoption, he launched GECTO in early 2026 with a clear mission: to bring modern technology to underserved businesses and individuals. Stenford is a self‑taught technologist, visionary leader, and passionate advocate for digital inclusion. He believes that no business should rely on paper books in the 21st century. Under his leadership, GECTO has already delivered multiple successful projects and built a growing team of developers, designers, and strategists. His bold "One Digitalised World" project aims to digitise Zimbabwe’s economy by 2032. Stenford is also committed to youth empowerment through SkillForge games that teach entrepreneurship and coding. He is frequently invited to speak at tech conferences and works closely with local municipalities to drive digital literacy. Mr Ceo's dream is to see every African child have access to a computer and every business operate online.

🌍 The "ONE DIGITALISED WORLD" Project

Launched in 2026 by founder Stenford Mpofu, this 7‑year initiative (2026–2032) aims to completely transform Zimbabwe into a digitally‑connected economy. The project focuses on moving traditional businesses – from market stalls to manufacturing plants – from paper records to cloud‑based databases, e‑commerce, and mobile applications. It targets underserved communities, providing affordable software, training, and ongoing technical support. By 2029, GECTO will establish a state‑of‑the‑art headquarters in Gwanda, turning the town into a Southern African tech innovation hub.

The "One Digitalised World" project includes building a network of 50 digital hubs in rural areas, offering free basic websites to 1,000 small businesses, and integrating SkillForge gamified learning into 200 schools. We are partnering with local municipalities, educational institutions, and private sector leaders to ensure no one is left behind. The project also includes a digital literacy campaign for adults, teaching online banking, e‑government services, and remote work skills.

By 2032, we envision a Zimbabwe where every citizen can access public services online, every business accepts digital payments, and every young person has a pathway to a tech career. This blueprint will be shared across Africa, empowering other nations to replicate the model. Already, we have secured initial funding and pilot projects in Bulawayo and Gwanda. The project also includes a sustainability arm – the GECTO Digital Legacy Fund – which will reinvest profits into free digitisation for vulnerable communities.

We invite investors, partners, and volunteers to join this historic movement. Together, we can build a future where geography is no longer a barrier to opportunity. The "One Digitalised World" is not just a slogan – it is a promise.
